## Field-by-field draft

### AIDA sentence (textarea, required)

Atomic, Independent, Declarative, Absolute. One empirical finding. Must end with a full stop.

```
Icechunk-backed transactional stores produce zero observable metadata–data
inconsistencies under writer failure and concurrent access, whereas disconnected STAC
metadata indexes over object storage produce such inconsistencies without external
orchestration.
```

> **Split-vs-combine note:** SCOPE.md §1 names two co-dependent conditions that both
> must hold for the claim to stand. Here they are kept as one comparative AIDA because
> the comparison is the unit of the finding — confirming one side without the other is
> not a meaningful result. If the platform or a reviewer requires strict atomicity, split
> into:
>
> - AIDA-A: "Icechunk-backed transactional stores produce zero observable metadata–data
>   inconsistencies under writer failure and concurrent access."
> - AIDA-B: "Disconnected STAC metadata indexes over object storage produce observable
>   metadata–data inconsistencies under writer failure and concurrent access without
>   external orchestration."
>
> Each AIDA-A / AIDA-B would then anchor its own FORRT Claim → Study → Outcome chain.
